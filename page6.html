What Cuisine Are You Eating?

    Things to Avoid:
  • Noodles (which may be coated with egg)
  • Fried foods (which may have egg in the dough)
    • Good Options:
  • Steamed veggies and chicken with rice (be sure to ask if the rice is cooked with eggs)
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Ask the chef to clean the wok before cooking your food to avoid cross-contamination.
  • Ask if their rice is cooked with eggs.
Traditional Korean food is unlikely to include eggs! But be sure to ask the chef about possible sources of eggs in your meal.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Fried food (due to eggs in the batter)
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Some sushi may have pieces of egg sprinkled on top.
  • Ask the chef to clean the pan before cooking your food to avoid cross-contamination.
Traditional Indian food is unlikely to include eggs! But be sure to ask the chef about possible sources of eggs in your meal.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Tagliatelli, fettuccine, taglionli, and pappardelle pasta (often made with egg)
  • Pizza (dough may contain eggs)
    • Good Options:
  • Spaghetti, penne, and farfalle pasta
  • Meat-based dishes
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Egg may be used when making many pastas from scratch, so be sure to ask the chef about the ingredients used to make the pasta you are considering.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Many baked goods
  • Pizza (dough may contain eggs)
  • Many types of bread
Most traditional Mexican entrees do not contain eggs. Be aware of desserts though, as they often contain eggs, and be sure to ask the chef about any menu options you are considering.
Items containing eggs are usually obviously labeled.
Most baked goods contain eggs, so ask the baker about egg-less options.
