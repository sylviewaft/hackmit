What Cuisine Are You Eating?

    Things to Avoid:
  • White rice
  • Fried dumplings, veggies, fish, etc.
    • Good Options:
  • Brown rice
  • Steamed dumplings, veggies, fish, etc.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Fried food
    • Good Options:
  • Tofu
  • Kimchi
  • Sauerkraut
  • Bibimbap (a rice dish with veggies and sometimes egg, ask for brown rice)
  • Kimbap (rice with dried seaweed, ask for brown rice)
Sushi is wrapped in white rice, so ask if brown rice can be substituted instead or stick to a small portion size.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Fried food
  • White rice
    • Good Options:
  • Lentil soup
  • Dal (lentil stew)
  • Chana masala (spicy chickpeas)
  • Tandoori chicken
    Things to Avoid:
  • Pasta-centered dishes
    • Good Options:
  • Meat-centered dishes with a side of pasta
  • Salads
    Things to Avoid:
  • Sugar
  • Pasta
  • White bread and white rice
  • Red Meats
    • Good Options:
  • Complex carbohydrates (brown rice, whole wheat, quinoa, oatmeal, beans, etc.)
  • Protein (beans, eggs, seafood, dairy, tofu, lean meats)
    Things to Avoid:
  • Tortillas
  • White rice
  • Chips & salsa
    • Good Options:
  • Burrito bowls
  • Grilled chicken or fish tacos
The Mediterranean diet is high in vegetables, fruits, and fish/seafood and low in red meat, which is generally good for those with diabetes as it can lower blood pressure.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Foods with a high sugar content
    • Good Options:
  • Substitute butter with avocado or applesauce
  • Use nut flours instead of wheat flours
  • Use cacao nibs instead of chocolate
