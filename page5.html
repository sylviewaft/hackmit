What Cuisine Are You Eating?

Traditional Chinese food is very likely to be dairy-free! But be sure to ask about potential sources of dairy.
Traditional Korean food is very likely to be dairy-free! But be sure to ask about potential sources of dairy.
Traditional Japanese food is very likely to be dairy-free! But be sure to ask about potential sources of dairy.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Most desserts
  • Raitas
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
    • Good Options:
  • Sag aloo
  • Chana masala
  • Egg burjli
  • Samosasa
  • Breads
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Milk is very commonly used in Indian food, so be sure to ask the chef about any menu item you’re interested in. Especially ask about the presence of ghee, which is used in many different meals.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Creamy sauces
    • Good Options:
  • Tomato-based sauces
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Cheese or milk may be added to menu items without being noted on the menu, so be sure to ask the chef about whatever menu item you’re interested in.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Many baked goods
  • Yogurt
  • Creamy sauces
  • Some salad dressings (may contain milk or butter)
  • Mashed potatoes
  • Creamed vegetables or soups
    • Good Options:
  • Rice, almond, soy, oat, or cashew milk
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Ask the chef about special dairy-free options they may have. Be sure to ask about hidden dairy-containing ingredients in any menu item you are considering.
Most traditional Mexican foods are dairy-free without the addition of cheese and sour cream as toppings. Be sure to make the chef aware of your dietary restrictions, and request your menu item without cheese and sour cream.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Paella
  • Lasagna
  • Cheese/yogurt
    • Good Options:
  • Unsweetened almond or soy milk
  • Milk is generally not a large part of the Mediterranean diet
    Things to Avoid:
  • Butter, Cream, milk, whey protein, caramel color or flavor, sour cream, whipped cream, cheese, and buttermilk
    • Good Options:
  • Seed/nut milk
  • Coconut oil/milk instead of butter/cream
  • Nut cheese instead of cheese
  • Dairy-free yogurt
  • Vegan buttermilk
