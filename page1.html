What Cuisine Are You Eating?

    Things to Avoid:
  • Soy sauce (you can bring a bottle of your own gluten-free brand with you from home)
  • Fish sauce, duck sauce, oyster sauce, teriyaki sauce and other dark sauces which may contain flour
  • Dumplings
  • Fried foods (due to cross-contamination in the fryer)
    • Good Options:
  • White sauces made with cornstarch
  • Rice & rice noodles (chow fun & mei fun). Avoid other types of noodles.
  • Steamed vegetables and chicken with rice
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Make sure the chef understands they need to clean the wok before cooking your food to avoid cross-contamination.

  • Pasta

    Things to Avoid:
  • French Fries (may be cooked in peanut oil)
  • Oats (are often grown in close proximity to wheat causing cross contamination)
  • Fried foods (due to cross contamination)
  • Hidden flour sources (barely, dextrin, malt, brown rice syrup)
  • Meatballs
  • Soy sauce
  • Sauces with flour used as thickening agent (ex: cream of mushroom soup)
    • Good Options:
  • Buckwheat Flour
  • Brown Rice Flour
  • Corn starch (as a thickening agent)
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Always ask about hidden sources of gluten in the menu option you are considering.
  • Ask the chef to ensure your food is cooked on a clean frying pan or grill.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Burritos
  • Chile Relleno
  • Chips & Salsa (corn tortilla chips may be fried with gluten-containing foods)
  • Sauces thickened with flour
    • Good Options:
  • Enchiladas made with corn tortillas
  • Fajitas made with corn tortillas
  • Tacos made with corn tortillas
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Always ask about possible sources of hidden gluten in your meal, and be sure to specify that you need a corn tortilla in place of a flour tortilla.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Pita bread
  • Couscous, farro
  • Pasta
  • Paella
    • Good Options:
  • A mediterranean diet is generally good for those with Celiac disease
  • Grains, fish, and veggies
  • Millet, Brown Rice, Quinoa, Buckwheat, whole grain cornmeal, and nut flours
Most foods sold in a normal bakery will not be gluten free. It may be useful to search for a dedicated gluten-free bakery.
