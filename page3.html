What Cuisine Are You Eating?

    Things to Avoid:
  • Egg rolls (they may be sealed with peanut butter)
  • Fried foods (which may be fried in peanut oil)
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Chinese restaurants use lots of peanuts and cashews in their meals, so be sure to ask the chef to clean the wok before cooking your food to avoid cross-contamination and ask about potential sources of nuts in your meal.
    Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Korean food generally doesn’t involve peanuts or peanut sauce! But be sure to ask about possible allergens.
    Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Most traditional Japanese food will not have nuts, including peanuts or peanut oil! But be sure to ask the chef about possible sources of nuts in your meal.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Fried foods (may be fried in peanut oil)
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Ask about possible nut contaminations, even if nuts aren’t listed in the menu description, as Indian food commonly contains nuts.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Pesto sauce (contains pine nuts)
  • Pastries (may contain ground nuts)
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Some Italian dishes may include ground nuts which are indistinguishable to the human eye. Be sure to ask the chef if a dish contains nuts before ordering.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Cereals and granolas with nuts in ingredients
  • Chili and soups with peanuts or peanut butter used as thickener
  • High energy bars or drinks (often contain peanuts)
  • Veggie burgers (often contain peanuts)
  • Salads or salad dressings with nuts
    Things to Avoid:
  • Mole Sauce
  • Mexican Pesto
  • Chile Relleno
  • Picadillo
  • Most desserts
    • Good Options:
  • Most main dishes, as traditional Mexican entrees normally don’t contain nuts.
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Be sure to ask about possible sources of cross-contamination from desserts containing nuts made in the same facility.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Baklava (contains walnuts)
    • Good Options:
  • Plant-based seeds
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Nuts are commonly used in Mediterranean diet, so be sure to ask the chef before consumption.
    Things to Avoid:
  • Macaroons (contain almond flour)
  • Ground nuts inside pastries not obviously visible
    • Good Options:
  • Oats, seeds, & Granola
  • Crisp rice
    • Questions to Ask the Chef:
  • Be sure to ask about possible sources of cross contamination.
